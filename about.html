About

Rory Kwan(they/she) is a BFA student at the University of Michigan, Class of 2024. Their career goals include working in the animation industry as a story artist or character designer. She also sees herself as an art teacher who makes comics and freelances on the side. Their dream job would be to start their own animation studio and direct movies/create TV shows.

Their art practice is guided by their passion for mindful representation. They strive to break harmful cycles with not only their storytelling but with their drawing style/character designs as well. They want their work to move all kinds of people and help raise more considerate and open-minded future generations. Some issues that drive their work include the climate crisis, animal welfare, racism/colorism, queerphobia, and fatphobia.

Rory also hopes their work will help people find the magic in life. They believe in being aware and active in fixing the issues of the world, but as humans, we must balance finding happiness for ourselves with facing the burdens of the society we live in. It is a balance she struggles with herself, and she hopes that her art can help others find magic, wonder, and light when the weight of the world feels like too much.
